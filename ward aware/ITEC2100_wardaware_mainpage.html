ITEC2100A - Ward Aware



Motivation

Our interactive data visualization would aid any newcomers to Ottawa. Our audience ranges from families, students, or any person who is interested in moving to Ottawa. Our data has information about Ottawa wards, neighbourhoods in the wards, parks and greenspaces, and transportation, all of which we deemed was some of the most important information that you would need when moving to a new area.



How to navigate the webpage

The main webpage (the page you are currently on) has visualizations that contain data for all of the wards in Ottawa. There are visualizations for average rent, average population, green space, and major transit stations. Explore the data on the main page first and when you find a ward that you are interested in, go to the scroll menu on the left and click on the ward you wish to explore more. It will take you to a webpage that has information only for the specific ward you chose(due to the time limitation, we were only able to make a individual webpage for Somerset).



Warning

1: When you first load the webpage, it will show all the visualizations because we could not figure out how to only show one visualization when it loads. Therefore, to please click on one of the filter buttons to make the website easier to navigate.

2: In the initial plan, users would have been able to click on the ward Somerset on the map to view the Somerset page. Due to time limitations, we were unable to figure this out and users should scroll on the bar on the left to access the Somerset page through the hyperlink

Conclusion

To summerize, we were able to create the interactive choropleth maps (for population and average rent), and interactive vertical bar graphs that show data on average rent, population, types of parks and greenspaces, and transportation (specifically o-trains and major bus stops). Although we were able to make most of what we had initially planned to make, we ran into some difficulties because we could not figure out how to code certin things. If we had more time, making indivisual websites for all the wards and linking the map visualization and bar chart visualization would have been the next step. Overall, we were able to make interactive visualizations that would aid any newcomers to Ottawa, and we hope it was useful for finding a new place to live in Ottawa!
